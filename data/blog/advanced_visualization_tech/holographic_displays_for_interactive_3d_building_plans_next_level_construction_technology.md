# Introduction

Delving into the future of building plans, **holographic displays** are now a popular disruption in the construction industry. Known for their interactive three-dimensional (3D) data visualization advantage, these cutting-edge systems allow architects, engineers, and developers to interact with digital representations of their building plans in a realistic 3D space.

## The Science Behind Holography

Holography originated from the field of physics and it involves the use of laser light to develop a fully three-dimensional image of a physical object. Unlike traditional 3D imaging techniques, holography enables a truly three-dimensional visual by preserving the complete depth information from the scene. This depth is what allows construction professionals to interact with 3D building plans as if they were physical objects.

## Evolution of Architectural Design 

Historically, architectural design has evolved from hand-drawn sketches to sophisticated CAD files. With the advent of holography, architectural design is set to evolve further:

1. **2D paper drawings**: Traditional blueprint that gives a top-down view of the layout.
2. **Computer-Aided Design (CAD)**: Digital 2D or 3D design that can be rotated and zoomed.
3. **Virtual Reality (VR)**: Surrounds architects in the building design but requires cumbersome equipment.
4. **Holographic Display**: Interactions with 3D building plans in a space that's visible to all viewers with depth-perception preserved.

# Benefits of Holographic Displays in Construction 

The integration of holographic displays into the construction industry offers several benefits:

- **Interactive Design Process**: One can directly interact with the designs, make appropriate adjustments, and visualize their impacts in real time.

- **Enhanced Communication**: It breaks the barrier of misunderstood blueprints, enhancing design communication and collaboration among team members.

- **Reduced Errors**: Real-time interaction with the building model reduces the risk of errors and revisions in the later stages of the project, saving time and costs. 

- **Improved Decision Making**: It provides a comprehensive view of the project, assisting stakeholders in understanding the project better and making informed decisions. 

## Case Study: HoloBuilder 

HoloBuilder, a construction technology company, introduced a holographic solution known as 'JobWalk'. This solution allows engineers and architects to have a 3D, interactive overview of the building plan that can be easily manipulated for fine tuning.

This table shows some key features of the HoloBuilder JobWalk:

| Feature | Description |
| ------- | ----------- |
| **360° Capture**         | Allows users to capture 360° images from any angle within the site. |
| **Time Travel**          | Enables users to compare construction progress over a duration. |
| **Offline Mode**         | Lets users to work remotely even without the internet. |

Overall, holographic displays and their applications in interactive 3D building plans are revolutionising the construction industry by providing architects and developers with advanced visualization techniques. This innovative technological shift is likely to drive the industry towards more efficient and effective construction processes.
