# Introduction
Surface degradation, primarily through corrosion, is a significant challenge in harsh environments. This issue affects various sectors like oil and gas extraction, shipping, and infrastructural developments, which all have massive financial implications. Nano-engineered coatings (NEC) have emerged as a robust solution to cope with these harsh environments. 

This tutorial will provide insight into:
- The science behind the NEC
- Methods to apply them
- Practical examples of their use
- Potential challenges in implementation

# Science Behind Nano-Engineered Coatings

Nano-engineering manipulates atomic, molecular, and supramolecular matter to derive coatings with far superior properties than traditional variants. Nano-coatings can provide extreme hardness, self-healing abilities, high resistance to wear, and superior corrosion protection.

# Applying Nano-Engineered Coatings

Application procedures for NEC can vary based on the composition of the coating and the required thickness. The common methods include:

1. **Electroplating**: Involves the reduction of metallic ions in a solution to form a metal coating.


2. **Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD)**: Involves conventional CVD processes to form a nano-ceramic based coating on a substrate.

3. **Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD)**: This is a vapor phase technique capable of producing thin films using sequential self-limiting surface reactions.

4. **Nano-Spray Coating**: Involves spraying nano-composite coatings in harsh environments to reduce downtime and increase convenience.

# Application Examples

## Oil & Gas Industry
Coating pipelines with NECs can enhance durability and longevity, preventing leaks and downtime due to maintenance.

## Infrastructure
NECs provide preventive maintenance on infrastructures in marine environments, reducing the long-term maintenance cost greatly.

## Shipping
Surface coatings on ships can reduce biofouling - the accumulation of microorganisms, plants, algae, or small animals on wet surfaces.

# Challenges 

Despite NECs' vast potential, several challenges persist:
- **Cost**: The high cost of nano-engineered coatings might deter their usage in industries with budget constraints.
- **Standardisation**: NECs are difficult to standardize due to the vast range of possibilities in their composition.
- **Environmental Impact**: NEC's environmental impact, if any, is not yet fully understood and needs robust investigation.

# Conclusion

The beneficial properties of Nano-Engineered Coatings make them a promising solution for corrosion protection in harsh environments. However, potential challenges must be responsibly addressed through continued research and standardization before their widespread adoption.
