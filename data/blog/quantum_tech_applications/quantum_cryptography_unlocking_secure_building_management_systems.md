# Introduction

Over the years, the concept of security in Building Management Systems (BMS) has evolved from simple lock-and-key mechanisms to complex, digitally managed systems. Today, advancements such as Quantum Cryptography present the opportunity to surpass traditional cryptographic methods, further bolstering the security of BMS.

## Quantum Cryptography: An Overview

Quantum Cryptography leverages principles of quantum mechanics to secure communication, making it unsusceptible to hacking or any form of eavesdropping. It features quantum key distribution (QKD), a procedure that uses quantum states to produce and distribute cryptographic key materials.

## Quantum Cryptography in Building Management Systems: A Promising Prospect

However futuristic it may seem, the application of Quantum Cryptography in BMS is on the horizon and promises substantial benefits:

1. **Enhanced Security**: The potential use of Quantum Cryptography in BMS could detect and neutralize external threats instantaneously, ensuring data integrity and system resilience.
2. **Scalability**: With QKD, cryptographic keys can be securely produced and distributed among a vast network of buildings, making it a scalable solution.

### Quantum Cryptography Vs. Traditional Cryptography

|              | Quantum Cryptography | Traditional Cryptography |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| Eavesdropping        | Automatically detects and alerts   | May go undetected               |
| Key Distribution    | Extremely secure QKD  | Keys may be compromised   |
| Scalability          | Amenable to large networks| Limited to smaller systems |

## Challenges and Road Ahead 

Just as with any other advanced technologies, Quantum Cryptography faces its fair share of challenges. The primary one being the difficulty in integrating quantum systems within conventional digital frameworks. While there is progress, much research and development are needed before Quantum Cryptography becomes a regular feature in BMS.
