# Introduction

The game-changing advent of 3D printing technology means not only a revolution for small-scale fabrications but also a paradigm shift within the construction industry. This article will offer a deep dive into the current state and projected trends in the field of automated 3D printing of large-scale construction elements. 

## The Emergence of 3D Printing in Construction

Three-dimensional, or 3D, printing is a process of building an object layer by layer from computer-aided design (CAD) data. The technology has found its way into the construction industry, where it presents a powerful tool for manufacturing large-scale elements to significantly enhance efficiency, reduce waste, and provide architectural flexibility.

# Prerequisites

1. Knowledge about 3D printing and Additive Manufacturing processes.
2. Familiarity with the basic principles of construction technology.
3. Basic concepts of CAD and Design Software.

# Steps towards Automated 3D Printing of Large-Scale Construction Elements

## Step 1: Design Phase using CAD Software

The first step in the automated 3D printing process involves creating a 3D model of the construction element to be printed. This step is typically completed using CAD software, which allows an architect or designer to construct a digital representation of the object.

```code
Example of a simplified CAD code:

# Create a new sketch
sketch = Part.newSketch('base sketch')
# Draw a rectangle to represent our building element
rectangle = sketch.createRectangle(10, 20, 0, 0)
# Finish the sketch
sketch.finishEdit()
```

## Step 2: Convert Design to STL Files

The next step is to "slice" the design into thin layers for printing. This process also converts the CAD design into an STL (Standard Tessellation Language) format, which the 3D printer uses to generate physical entities.

## Step 3: Material Preparation

In this step, the construction material is prepared. This could typically involve a special type of concrete that is optimized for 3D printing, having the right viscosity and setting characteristics that allow it to be extruded through the 3D printer's nozzle, while also maintaining its shape once layered.

## Step 4: Printing Process

The 3D printer reads the STL file and, guided by the robotic arm, starts extruding the prepared concrete in a layer-by-layer process. The printer keeps adding these layers on top of one another, adhering to the previous layer, until the desired object is reproduced physically.

# Best Practices

1. Thorough structural analysis should be conducted on the CAD model before printing.
2. Material preparation is crucial. Ensure the concrete's viscosity and curing time are appropriate for your project.
3. Routine maintenance and calibration of the 3D printer and robot arm is essential in ensuring smooth operation and desired printing quality.

# Potential Challenges

1. **Temperature Control** - The curing process of the 3D printed concrete can be significantly affected by the environmental temperature.
2. **Material Consistency** - Construction materials used in 3D printing need to possess specific properties to ensure their application in the construction field is successful.
3. **Size Restrictions** - While there are printers capable of printing large structures, there's still confinement surrounding the size of objects that can be 3D printed in one piece.

# Conclusion

Automated 3D printing in construction promises a radical transformation of the industry. However, it also comes with its own set of unique challenges. Only by developing a comprehensive understanding of the process, potential difficulties, and evolving technology, one can fully leverage the benefits offered by this trailblazing technology. This tutorial provides a solid foundation for anyone looking to delve into the realm of automated 3D printing in the construction sector.
