# Bioengineered Self-Repairing Concrete: A Pioneering Contribution to Biotech in Construction

The construction industry forms the backbone of our infrastructure, shaping our cities and everything within them. As the world develops, our infrastructure has to keep up, and traditional materials such as concrete are being modified radically with the aid of technology. One triumph in this frontier is bioengineered self-repairing concrete, or more excitingly, living concrete that has the ability to repair its own cracks.

## The Problem with Traditional Concrete

Every construction expert knows that although concrete is an essential material for infrastructure, it isn't perfect. With time, cracking is inevitable leading to decrease in the lifespan of the structure and increased maintenance costs. 

Here's a glimpse of the common concrete problems:

- Cracks due to thermal contraction and stress
- Water and pollutant penetration leading to decreased durability
- Costly maintenance and repair procedures
- Contributing to environmental degradation

## The Bioengineering Solution

The solution lies in the application of bioengineering in the concrete mix. Scientists have discovered that certain types of bacteria, specifically _Bacillus Pseudofirmus_ and _Sporosarcina Pasteurii_, have the ability to produce limestone. When added to the concrete mix, these bacteria can lie dormant for years until the concrete begins to crack and water seeps in. This triggers the bacteria to proliferate and start producing limestone, which in turn repairs the cracks.

Here's an outline using markdown lists to elucidate the process:

1. Bacteria along with calcium lactate are mixed into the concrete.
2. These bacteria can remain dormant for up to 200 years within the concrete mix.
3. When the concrete cracks, water and oxygen seep in, which awakens the bacteria.
4. The bacteria start to consume the calcium lactate, and in the process produce limestone which fills in the cracks.

## Benefits of Bioengineered Self-Repairing Concrete

This innovative solution brings multiple benefits, as illustrated in the table below:

| Benefits       | Description |
| -------------- | ----------- |
| **Sustainability** | As the concrete can repair its own cracks, it leads to lower carbon emissions and waste creation in the repair and replacement of concrete structures. |
| **Increased Lifespan** | By self-repairing its cracks, the concrete's durability is greatly increased and the lifespan of the structure is extended. |
| **Cost-effectiveness** | It reduces costs associated with maintenance and replacement of parts of or entire structures. |
| **Resiliency** | Micro-cracks are fixed immediately, maintaining the structure's strength and resistance against external disruptions. |

## Future Implications

With rapid advances in the field of biotech in construction, bioengineered self-repairing concrete is promisingly just the proverbial tip of the iceberg. The use of bacteria for self-repairing concrete is paving the way for a new approach in construction that indirectly contributes to environmental sustainability while still fulfilling our need for reliable and robust infrastructure.

With more research, the possibilities are exponential, implicating not just our buildings, but our roads, bridges and other concrete structures that are pivotal to modern civilization. Our urban landscapes could be populated with living buildings that are capable of regeneration leading to a new era of urban resilience and sustainable construction.

This breakthrough highlights the necessity for continued research and development in bioengineered solutions within the construction industry; a path that intertwines the survival of our planet with human advancement.
