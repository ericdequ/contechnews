# Self-Healing Materials in Construction: Reducing Maintenance Costs

Changes are happening on the front lines of the construction industry, spurred by the introduction of high-tech advanced materials, among which self-healing materials stand out prominently. As we continue to recognize the importance of sustainable construction, these materials represent an effective way of reducing maintenance costs and improving the lifespan of infrastructures.

## What are Self-Healing Materials?

Self-healing materials are designed to repair their cracks or damages without human intervention or external triggers. They imitate the natural healing process present in many organic systems.

```python
class SelfHealingMaterials:
    def __init__(self, self_healing_capacity):
        self.self_healing_capacity = self_healing_capacity

    def self_heal(self):
        return "Currently self-healing"
```

The example of a simple code above depicts the autonomous nature of self-healing materials.

## Types of Self-Healing Materials

Generally, two types of self-healing materials are used in construction:

- **Intrinsic**: The repair mechanism is tied into the material itself, usually triggered by a certain mechanical, thermal or optical stimulus.
- **Extrinsic**: The healing agents are contained in a separate compartment or network of vessels within the material and are released in case of damage.

|Types of Self-healing Materials|Pros|Cons|
|---|---|---|
|Intrinsic|Inherent self-healing capability.|Sometimes require external stimulus.|
|Extrinsic|Flexibility to manipulate the healing agent.|Risk of exhausting the healing agent.|

## Self-Healing Materials and Maintenance Cost

Maintenance costs are a significant expenditure in the construction industry, and the advent of self-healing materials could lead to substantial cost savings. Here's how:

1. **Reducing Repair Costs**: With the capacity for autonomous healing, these materials require fewer repairs, reducing the frequency of maintenance.
2. **Increasing Lifespan**: By healing themselves, the materials ensure longevity, delaying replacements and in turn, saving costs.
3. **Workforce Reduction**: Self-reparation is autonomous, thus reducing the need for additional labor.
